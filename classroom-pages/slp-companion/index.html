Step 2 of 5

The Expansion Decision

Read the situation below and choose the move you would most likely make. There is no perfect answer. Each option reveals a different leadership instinct.

Maya is a Senior Operations Manager at a mid-sized logistics company. She reports directly to the CEO.

For the past two quarters, the CEO has been pushing for the business to expand into three new markets at the same time. The board likes the ambition. The sales team sees upside. The CEO believes the timing is right.

But Maya is seeing different signals. Her team is already stretched. Two strong performers have quietly raised burnout concerns. Recent margin analysis shows the core market still has untapped opportunity. Customer behaviour is also shifting: clients are asking for more flexible delivery models, not just broader geographic coverage.

Maya is not against growth. But she is concerned that the current plan is too broad, too fast, and may distract the business from the future it actually needs to prepare for.

The CEO has scheduled a strategy review for Friday. The meeting will include the CEO, CFO, Sales Director, and two board members. Maya has the data, but she also knows the CEO is emotionally committed to the expansion narrative.
